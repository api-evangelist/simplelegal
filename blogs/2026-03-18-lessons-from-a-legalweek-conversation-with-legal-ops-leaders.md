<p>Innovation Is Easy. Execution Is Hard.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Legal departments have never had more technology available to them.&nbsp;</p>



<p>AI tools.&nbsp;Workflow automation.&nbsp;Advanced analytics.&nbsp;Unified legal platforms.&nbsp;</p>



<p>And yet transformation still stalls.&nbsp;</p>



<p>At&nbsp;Legalweek, Onit’s <a href="https://linkedin.com/in/jeffrey-solomon-a8b67b31/">Jeffrey Solomon</a> sat down with two legal operations leaders who know this problem well:&nbsp;</p>



<p>Jasmine Sims, VP, Global Legal Ops at IBM&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Kim Wolfe, Senior Vice President &#8211; CAO for Legal and Head of Legal Operations, Contracts, and Innovation at State Street</p>



<p>The conversation&nbsp;wasn’t&nbsp;about the next tool.&nbsp;It was about something harder:&nbsp;executing innovation.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Problem&nbsp;Isn’t&nbsp;Technology</strong>&nbsp;</h2>



<p>Legal teams are investing heavily in systems designed to <a href="https://www.onit.com/blog/modern-legal-operations/">modernize operations</a>, but many of those initiatives struggle to gain traction.&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Adoption slows.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Workflows revert to old habits.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The new platform becomes another system people work around.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<p>Not because the technology is flawed.&nbsp;Because the organization&nbsp;wasn’t&nbsp;ready.&nbsp;</p>



<p>As the panel made clear, the biggest barrier to transformation in legal operations is rarely technical-&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;operational.&nbsp;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="legal operations" class="wp-image-18049" height="536" src="https://www.onit.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/performance-data-1024x536.png" width="1024" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Leaders Who Succeed Ask Different Questions</strong>&nbsp;</h2>



<p>Most teams begin&nbsp;transformation&nbsp;the same way.&nbsp;“What technology should we buy?”&nbsp;</p>



<p>But the most effective legal ops leaders start somewhere else.&nbsp;They ask:&nbsp;“Is our organization ready to use it?”&nbsp;</p>



<p>That question changes everything.&nbsp;It forces leaders to understand:&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Where work breaks&nbsp;down.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Where decisions slow&nbsp;down.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Where legal and the business fall&nbsp;out of sync.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<p>Before any automation happens&nbsp;or&nbsp;any platform goes live.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Start With Listening</strong>&nbsp;</h2>



<p>Kim Wolfe explained that transformation in legal operations begins with understanding people.&nbsp;Every legal organization is different.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Different GCs.&nbsp;Different priorities.&nbsp;Different risk tolerances.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Solutions built without that context rarely stick.&nbsp;The work starts with listening.&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Where are the real friction points?&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Where does&nbsp;legal&nbsp;spend too much time?&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Where do business partners feel the pain?&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<p>Only once those answers are clear does technology become useful.&nbsp;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img alt="" class="wp-image-18033" height="333" src="https://www.onit.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/brown-and-white-wooden-round-frame.png" style="width: 840px; height: auto;" width="500" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Fix the Process Before the Platform</strong>&nbsp;</h2>



<p>Another mistake legal teams often&nbsp;make:&nbsp;<a href="https://www.onit.com/blog/disconnected-legal-workflows/">automating a broken process</a>.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Jasmine Sims put it plainly during the discussion.&nbsp;</p>



<p>When budgets are tight, the fastest way to unlock technology investment is to fix inefficient processes first.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Because good technology cannot repair a bad process.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Legal ops leaders who understand this sequence focus on operational clarity first.&nbsp;Then they automate.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Where AI Actually Helps</strong>&nbsp;</h2>



<p>There’s&nbsp;another assumption that slows progress in legal departments.&nbsp;That AI will replace lawyers.&nbsp;</p>



<p>It&nbsp;won’t.&nbsp;</p>



<p>The legal profession runs on judgment.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Lawyers interpret context.&nbsp;Assess&nbsp;risk.&nbsp;Make decisions with accountability.&nbsp;</p>



<p>AI does something different.&nbsp;It removes the low-judgment work.&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Reviewing standard clauses.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Scanning large contract portfolios.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Identifying&nbsp;patterns across thousands of documents.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<p>That’s&nbsp;<a href="https://www.onit.com/blog/generative-ai-legal-teams/">where AI shines</a>.&nbsp;Humans define the&nbsp;decisions,&nbsp;AI helps them get there faster&nbsp;while&nbsp;still allowing&nbsp;them the oversight that keeps them comfortable.&nbsp;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="" class="wp-image-18039" height="684" src="https://www.onit.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Screenshot-2026-03-17-131721-1024x684.png" width="1024" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Build the Foundation First</strong>&nbsp;</h2>



<p>The biggest takeaway from <a href="https://www.event.law.com/legalweek">the&nbsp;Legalweek&nbsp;conversation</a> was simple.&nbsp;</p>



<p>The organizations that benefit most from AI will not be the ones that deploy it&nbsp;first,&nbsp;they will be the ones that prepare for it.&nbsp;</p>



<p>That preparation looks like operational maturity:&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Asking&nbsp;better questions about&nbsp;processes and&nbsp;workflows.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Governing how decisions&nbsp;are&nbsp;executed.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Automating the work that slows teams down.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<p>When those elements come together, legal operations&nbsp;stops&nbsp;being a reporting function&nbsp;and&nbsp;becomes something more powerful&nbsp;&#8211; a&nbsp;system of execution.&nbsp;</p>



<p>And that’s where real transformation begins.&nbsp;</p>
