<p><sup>Updated April 2026</sup></p>



<p>The Uniform Task-Based Management System (<a href="https://utbms.com/">UTBMS</a>) details a series of code sets that law firms use to classify services on electronic invoices sent to clients, such as legal operations and corporate legal departments.</p>



<p>UTBMS codes make detailed spend reporting possible by ensuring that each task and expense is categorized. That way, when you notice in Q1 that spending is out of control, you&#8217;re able to identify and solve the problem before Q4. Of course, in order for reporting to be accurate, you must first fully understand UTBMS codes and how to use them.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What are UTBMS codes?</strong></h2>



<p>UTBMS codes are a set of codes originally developed by the <a href="https://www.americanbar.org/">American Bar Association</a> (ABA), the <a href="https://www.acc.com/">Association of Corporate Counsel</a> (ACC), and <a href="https://www.pwc.com/">PricewaterhouseCoopers</a> (PwC). UTBMS standards are now maintained by the Legal Electronic Data Exchange Standard (LEDES) Oversight Committee, also known as LOC.</p>



<p>The creators designed UTBMS codes to standardize the categorization of legal services and expenses so that legal work and the associated costs could be easily identified and analyzed. You can learn more about the LEDES file format and LOC at <a href="https://utbms.com/">utbms.com</a>.</p>



<p>UTBMS codes are used in many legal systems around the world, including the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom. For this article, we will focus on UTBMS standards for e-Billing set by the ABA and LOC.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>American Bar Association UTBMS codes</strong></h2>



<p>When used consistently and properly, ABA UTBMS task codes allow you to monitor legal spending and associated activities.</p>



<p>All ABA UTBMS codes are broken into categories and phases. Categories are identified by the beginning letter (e.g., L for ABA litigation codes) while phases are specified by the number (100s for phase 1, 200s for phase 2, etc.). While some UTBMS codes are fairly self-explanatory, others require a deeper explanation. We&#8217;ll walk you through all of the <a href="https://www.americanbar.org/groups/litigation/resources/uniform_task_based_management_system/">ABA UTBMS codes</a>, and provide more insight when necessary.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Activity</strong></h3>



<p>Activity codes identify the type of activity associated with a cost.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>A101 Plan and prepare for</li>



<li>A102 Research</li>



<li>A103 Draft/revise</li>



<li>A104 Review/analyze</li>



<li>A105 Communicate (in firm)</li>



<li>A106 Communicate (with client)</li>



<li>A107 Communicate (other outside counsel)</li>



<li>A108 Communicate (other external)</li>



<li>A109 Appear for/attend</li>



<li>A110 Manage data/files</li>



<li>A111 Other</li>
</ul>



<p>Although not all clients require the use of UTBMS activity codes, this code set is useful for segmenting specific types of work. For instance, the four separate communication codes ensure more <strong>accurate spend tracking</strong> for both counsel and client. In situations where you may need to consult with an expert as well as outside counsel, codes A108 and A107, respectively, allow you to categorize time spent on each type of communication.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Bankruptcy</strong></h3>



<p>Derived from the code set published by the U.S. Department of Justice, bankruptcy UTBMS codes are intended only for bankruptcy matters. All adversarial tasks are covered by the litigation code set. The 21 bankruptcy ABA task codes are broken up into four phases.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>B100 Administration</strong></h3>



<p>UTBMS codes in the B100 phase include administrative work during preparation, such as research, fee applications, and communication with creditors.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>B110 Case Administration: Preparation of coordination and compliance matters, financial affairs statements, and general creditor inquiries</li>



<li>B120 Asset Analysis and Recovery: Identification and review of potential assets including causes of action and non-litigation recoveries</li>



<li>B130 Asset Disposition: Sales, abandonment and transaction work related to asset disposition</li>



<li>B140 Relief from Stay/Adequate Protection Proceedings: Matters relating to termination or continuation of automatic stay under 362 and motions for adequate protection</li>



<li>B150 Meetings of and Communications with Creditors: Preparing for and attending the conference of creditors, the 341(a) meeting and other creditors&#8217; committee meetings</li>



<li>B160 Fee/Employment Applications: Preparations of employment and fee applications for self or others; motions to establish interim procedures</li>



<li>B170 Fee/Employment Objections: Review of and objections to the employment and fee applications of others</li>



<li>B180 Avoidance Action Analysis: Review of potential avoiding actions under Sections 544–549 of the Code to determine whether adversary proceedings are warranted</li>



<li>B185 Assumption/Rejection of Leases and Contracts: Analysis of leases and executory contracts and preparation of motions specifically to assume or reject</li>



<li>B190 Other Contested Matters (excluding assumption/rejection motions): Analysis and preparation of all other motions, opposition to motions and reply memoranda in support of motions</li>



<li>B195 Non-Working Travel: Non-working travel where the court reimburses at less than full hourly rates</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>B200 Operations</strong></h3>



<p>B200 codes cover business matters, such as document review, employee benefits, cash collaterals, real estate, and tax issues.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>B210 Business Operations: Issues related to debtor-in-possession operating in Chapter 11 such as employee, vendor, and tenant issues and other similar problems</li>



<li>B220 Employee Benefits/Pensions: Review issues such as severance, retention, 401K coverage and continuance of pension plan</li>



<li>B230 Financing/Cash Collections: Matters under 361, 363 and 364 including cash collateral and secured claims; loan document analysis</li>



<li>B240 Tax Issues: Analyses and advice regarding tax-related issues, including the preservation of net operating loss carry forwards</li>



<li>B250 Real Estate: Review and analysis of real estate-related matters, including purchase agreements and lease provisions (e.g., common area maintenance clauses)</li>



<li>B260 Board of Directors Matters: Preparation of materials for and attendance at Board of Directors meetings; analysis and advice regarding corporate governance issues and review and preparation of corporate documents (e.g., Articles, Bylaws, employment agreements, compensation plans, etc.)</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>B300 Claims and plan</strong></h3>



<p>The B300 codes are used for all work related to claim inquiries and preparing disclosure statements and business plans.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>B310 Claims and Administration Objections: Specific claim inquiries; bar date motions; analyses, objections and allowances of claims</li>



<li>B320 Plan and Disclosure Statement (including Business Plan): Formulation, presentation and confirmation; compliance with the plan confirmation order, related orders and rules; disbursement and case closing activities, except those related to the allowance and objections to allowance of claims</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>B400 Bankruptcy-related advice</strong></h3>



<p>All advice, analyses, and consultations related to bankruptcy matters fall under the B400 code set.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>B410 General Bankruptcy Advice/Options: Analysis, advice and/or opinions regarding potential bankruptcy related issues, where no bankruptcy case has been filed</li>



<li>B420 Restructurings: Analysis, consultation and drafting in connection with the restructuring of agreements, including financing agreements, where no bankruptcy case has been filed</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Counseling</strong></h3>



<p>Counseling is one of the broader categories of UTBMS codes. This code set is designed to cover time used by attorneys preparing to provide legal advice. Generally, the counseling ABA task codes do not attribute time to a specific matter. Instead, they serve as a catchall billing code for time spent on research throughout a monthly billing period.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>C100 Fact Gathering: All initial inquiries, meetings, and data/information collection related to the assignment</li>



<li>C200 Researching Law: Time spent researching relevant case law or general investigation as well as consultations with experts</li>



<li>C300 Analysis and Advice: Analysis of work performed under C100 and C200 along with providing opinions and advice to client</li>



<li>C400 Third Party Communication: Discussions with third parties such as regulators or parties to contracts with the client</li>
</ul>

<p>This code set not only facilitates educated budget planning but also simplifies the process of submitting attorney expenses.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Project</strong></h3>



<p>For non-litigation matters, project codes are used for administrative filings, transactions, and stand-alone projects. The project code set includes eight phases.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>P100 Project Administration: All initial administration work such as developing, negotiating, and revising the plan and budget for the matter at hand</li>



<li>P200 Fact Gathering/Due Diligence</li>
</ul>



<p>Codes within the P200 phase are used for time spent on fact investigation, document retrieval, and preparation of reports with clients. They also cover coordination with third parties related to these activities.</p>



<p>Each P200 UTBMS code designates time spent on fact investigation/due diligence from a specific perspective, such as tax or environmental.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>P210 Corporate Review</li>



<li>P220 Tax</li>



<li>P230 Environmental</li>



<li>P240 Real and Personal Property</li>



<li>P250 Employee/Labor</li>



<li>P260 Intellectual Property</li>



<li>P270 Regulatory Reviews</li>



<li>P280 Other</li>
</ul>



<p>P300–P800 make up the additional codes within this phase.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>P300 Structure/Strategy/Analysis:</strong> Time spent on analysis done for the purposes of developing the strategy for a project or transaction. This includes all steps taken to create a written outline or description of the strategy</li>



<li><strong>P400 Initial Document Preparation/Filing:</strong> Tasks performed to prepare documents and opinions before being sent to third parties. This includes filing documents, related communications with the client, and review of client-generated transaction documentation</li>



<li><strong>P500 Negotiation/Revision/Responses:</strong> Time spent negotiating and revising P400 transaction documentation, including all related document review, meetings, and client communications</li>



<li><strong>P600 Completion/Closing:</strong> All tasks related to transaction pre-closing and closing, project completion or filing acceptance, such as attendance at closing</li>



<li><strong>P700 Post-Completion/Post Closing:</strong> All post-completion or post-closing tasks such as amendments to final documentation and resolution of post-closing issues. Also includes all implementation tasks (e.g., funds held in escrow) and preparation of closing binders (i.e., primarily clerical actions)</li>



<li><strong>P800 Maintenance and Renewal:</strong> All tasks related to subsequent maintenance and renewal requirements under the terms of the transaction or project such as monitoring of lease agreements, routine waivers and coordination of UCC requirements</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Workers&#8217; compensation</strong></h3>



<p>A 2010 ratification defined the workers&#8217; compensation code set. Many tasks include a &#8220;Commentary &amp; Practice Tips&#8221; subset that shares the same code as the parent task. For example, WC 110 could refer to Fact Investigation/Development or the Commentary &amp; Practice Tips related to that task.</p>



<p>This UTBMS code set includes a total of five phases and 27 ABA task codes. For the sake of brevity, we will provide a high-level description of each phase. It&#8217;s important to note that the Workers&#8217; Compensation code set does not include phase-level parent tasks (WC 100, WC 200, WC 300, etc.)</p>
